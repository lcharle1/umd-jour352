Sundown Towns in Maryland

Sundown towns, also known as sunset towns, gray towns, or sundowner towns, are typically all white in population and exclude a specific demographic. They are known throughout the United States and Canada. In America, they are well known for excluding African Americans, but some have segregated against Jews and others.

These towns operated between 1890 to 1968. The data speculates some towns could still be segregated or exclude minorities due to the lack of reported minority population in the area.

Each town had different methods of excluding or segregating unwanted demographics. From violence to economic coercion, the white population would find any means to exile or push away African Americans or other uninvited demographics.
